As I was walking home with a coffee in my hand, I was enjoing the autumn beauty of Bristol and being slightly nervous about not doing work at 11am.
“You’ll take a sick day if you don’t do anything today”, I kept telling myself.
As I was in my head thinking about work and how beautiful everything is, I saw a old short woman walking towards me. As I was approaching her, she started talking to me. I didn’t quite catch the beggining of it but it quickly became:
“… these people … narcotics … devil city … they don’t do anything about it”.
She’s been saying a lot of words with a thick slavic accent. Being caught by surprise and not expeting this conversation go beyond a casual two-phrase interaction, I didn’t catch most of the things she was saying. I guess I thought that at some point she’ll stop and I’ll have a chance to give her a generic responce to continue my morning in the blessed silence.
I was so naive, haha.
Hearing she’s talking about “narcotics”, I figured I need to start thinking of my way out of this conversation or I’ll be here for long.

“I don’t like the food in here. You go to the shop and there’s nothing good!”
“… my friend. She has problems with her partner. I don’t wanna talk about her personal things.”
“I went to the coffee shop, there were two: a man and a woman near me…”

She’s been jumping from one thing to another without a lot of transition. It really felt like an unfiltered stream of consciousness which has no end to it. Nothing she’s been saying could possibly reach any sort of logical end of conversation. To be honest, there were not much conversation there in the first place. Now I realised that I’m here for long. Still worrying about work, I decided to at least play along and try to enjoy this.

“They say Russia is bad but England is also bad!”
“See this church? It used to be a church, now it’s an appartment building … devil city!”
“Do you think it’s all goverment’s fault?”

As I went into it, I noticed that she’s not just ranting to me. Yes, she did complain, but she was genuine and kind and just felt like discussing life with a random person on the street. I couldn’t participate much in the conversation, so I just tried to listen, understand her way of thinking and appreciate her honesty and willing to talk to me. I guess I wouldn’t have noticed any of that if I continued trying to fing my way.

At the end she told me that someone asked her to buy sausages, which she hates and that her friend’s ex-partner doesn’t give her money for the kids. Whether it’s the the same friend who’s problems she didn’t want to disclose before, I don’t know. Then she said that she shouldn’t speak anymore and that she needs to go home.

“God gave us a chance to meet”, she said before saying goodbye.
“Indeed”, I thought, and said my goodbyes.

It’s so fascinating how easily old people can just speak with strangers on the street. I wish I could learn the same skill.

“I should write about this in my morning pages!”
Thanks grandma. God save you.
